Implementation for Strix Halo (Headless Linux)
Your Strix Halo acts as a "Local Cloud." While you have massive memory (128GB), your bottleneck is Memory Bandwidth (~270 GB/s vs 1,000+ GB/s on H100s).

Performance Expectation:

70B Models: Expect 5–8 tokens per second. This is faster than you can read, but not "instant."

Recommendation: Do not use Q8 (8-bit) quantization. It is too heavy for the bandwidth. Q5_K_M or Q6_K is the "sweet spot" for speed vs. intelligence.

1. Software Stack: llama.cpp On a headless AMD APU, llama.cpp is the most efficient runner. It can leverage the iGPU via Vulkan or ROCm, but even pure CPU inference will be decent with your AVX-512 support.
